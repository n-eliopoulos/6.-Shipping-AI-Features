Shipping AI Features

Module 2: Observability

Why AI Observability Is Different

Traditional services need three metrics: latency, error rate, and throughput. AI services need those plus an entirely new layer: token usage, prompt/response content, model version, confidence scores, and hallucination indicators. You need both layers to operate an AI feature in production.

The two-layer observability model

LayerMetricsWhy
Infrastructure (standard)Latency, error rate, throughput, CPU, memoryIs the service healthy?
AI-specificToken usage, cost per call, model version, confidence, prompt/response contentIs the AI behaving correctly?

What goes wrong without AI observability

  • Silent quality degradation. The model starts hallucinating more, but error rate stays at 0% because the API returns 200 OK.
  • Cost surprises. A prompt change doubles token usage. Without tracking, you find out on the monthly bill.
  • Debugging blind spots. A multi-step AI workflow fails. Without trace correlation, you can't tell which step went wrong.
  • Model drift. The provider updates the model. Your outputs change. Without content logging, you can't compare before/after.
Standard APM tools (Datadog, CloudWatch) cover the infrastructure layer. For the AI layer, you need additional instrumentation — structured logging of every AI call with token counts, costs, and content.
